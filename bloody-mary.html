Bloody Mary - the hearty classic

In the USA in particular, Bloody Mary is a popular drink for brunch and is considered the perfect counter-drink after a long night of drinking. In this country, however, the cocktail is often underestimated - most associate tomato juice with flights or fasting cures. However, if you mix the vegetable juice with fine vodka, fresh lemon, spicy-sweet Worceistershire sauce and Tabasco, the result is a great aperitif that you should definitely try.

How did the Bloody Mary get its name?

The naming of the "bloody cocktail" can be traced back to several stories. An American actor named George Jessel is often cited as the author. In 1927, after a long night of partying in a bar in Florida, he mixed the said drink from an opened bottle of vodka in order to be able to offer his guests a hangover drink. After a friend named Mary spilled the red drink on her white dress, George gave it the name "Bloody Mary".

Fernand Petiot, a famous bartender, is also considered the inventor of the classic Bloody Mary, which he had been preparing in a Manhattan bar since 1934.

Ingredients

  • For 1 glass
  • 5 cl vodka
  • 10 cl tomato juice
  • 1 dash of Worcestershire sauce
  • 1-2 dashes of Tabasco
  • Some salt and pepper from the mill
  • A good squeeze of lemon juice

Preparation

5 minutes

very easy

  1. Place all ingredients in a blender jug ​​filled with ice cubes and stir well.
  2. Pour the stirred mixture into a chilled glass with ice cubes.
  3. Garnish with a celery stick and serve.
