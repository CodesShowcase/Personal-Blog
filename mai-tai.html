Mai Tai - the South Seas classic

From the juices of fruity orange and pineapple, with a good dash of rum and grenadine and a lime, this cocktail classic will inspire you. Whether for a party, a girls' night out or a barbecue - a mai tai always goes with it!

Where does the name "Mai Tai" come from?

In the Tahitian language, the adjective maita'i means "good". However, this tiki cocktail was not created in Polynesia, but in the USA. The drink was born in a bar in California in 1944 when Victor Bergeron served it to two Tahitian friends. They were blown away and shouted Maita'i roa ae! which means something like "The best!" or "Out of this world!"

Ingredients

  • 1 lime
  • 30 ml white rum
  • 30 ml brown rum
  • 20 ml triple sec
  • 30 ml orange juice
  • 30 ml pineapple juice
  • 10 ml grenadine
  • Pineapple and mint for garnish
  • Crushed ice

Preparation

5 minutes

very easy

  1. Halve the lime and squeeze the juice.
  2. Vigorously shake the rum, triple sec, lime, orange and pineapple juice with crushed ice.
  3. Pour the grenadine into a glass, pour over the contents of the shaker.
  4. Decorate with pineapple and mint.
