Mojito is one of the most popular cocktail classics. The drink made of rum, sugar, lime juice, mint and soda water is wonderfully refreshing and brings a Cuban feeling straight into the glass!

How is a Mojito prepared?

Mojito originally comes from Havana, so white, Cuban rum is used for the classic preparation. By pressing the fine mint leaves, essential oils are released, which ensure a fresh aroma in the glass. Mojito is served in the classic way in a long drink glass.

You love the classic, but like to try something new? Then mix our delicious variants with melon, rhubarb or strawberries for more variety!

Ingredients

  • For 2 glasses (approx. 400 ml each)
  • 1 organic lime
  • 4 sprigs of mint
  • 2-4 tsp white cane sugar
  • 10 cl white rum (Cuban)
  • Crushed ice
  • 250ml soda water

Preparation

5 minutes

very easy

  1. Wash the lime in hot water, rub dry and cut into eighths. Wash mint and pat dry. Pluck the leaves from the stems, except for 2 stem tips.
  2. Put 4 pieces of lime and 2 teaspoons of sugar in each glass and crush lightly with a cocktail pestle. Measure 5 cl of rum with a bar measure and pour into the glass. Press the mint leaves lightly between your palms and spread over the glasses.
  3. Fill each glass 2/3 full with crushed ice and top up with 125ml soda water. Stir with a long spoon and decorate with the remaining mint.
